# Book 1: The Failed Summon — Remaining Book Outline

This outline covers **Chapters / Episodes 4–25**, following the drafted Chapters 1–3:

1. **The Lowest Fire** — Reina fails the fire-wisp practical and opens the road.
2. **The Man in the Circle** — Jake arrives, survives containment, and the bond is confirmed.
3. **Breakfast with the Black Familiar** — Tamsin brings Jake and Reina together for breakfast; the first fragile consent-based interaction begins.

The remainder of Book 1 should maintain the surface shape of an academy fantasy while steadily revealing that Veyrhold is built over old-road infrastructure, Reina's magic is structural rather than merely weak, Jake's body is an ancient guardian template, and the familiar bond creates intimacy that can become ethically intolerable.

---

## Through-Line for the Remainder of Book 1

### Visible Book Arc

Jake adjusts to Veyrhold Academy while Reina navigates social consequences, legal responsibility, magical scrutiny, and her increasingly complicated bond with him. The academy tries to define Jake as familiar, person, danger, divine omen, and institutional asset. Jake and Tamsin become romantically and physically involved, but the bond makes privacy impossible, causing Jake to break it off. The book ends with an attack on Veyrhold that exposes old circle infrastructure and leaves the academy, crown, church, and outside factions preparing for war.

### Hidden Book Arc

Reina's summoning was not a simple accident. Her Varros blood, Veyrhold's buried transit root, Zoe's intervention, War's interest, and Fire's mark made Jake's arrival possible. Book 1 quietly prepares the reader for the eventual Confluence twist by repeatedly associating circles with roads, roads with Earth, and Jake with an unclaimed guardian role.

### Relationship Arc

- Jake and Reina begin with guilt, fear, and unwanted emotional access.
- Tamsin becomes Jake's safest adult connection and eventual lover.
- Reina feels enough through the bond to become jealous, ashamed, and painfully aware that the bond is invasive.
- Jake breaks off physical intimacy with Tamsin after realizing Reina can feel parts of it.
- Reina begins learning that loving, needing, or summoning someone does not make them hers.

# Chapter / Episode 4 — Property with a Pulse

## Episode Function

Formalize the legal and ethical stakes of Jake's existence. The academy begins determining whether Jake is Reina's familiar, a person, a beast, a divine anomaly, or a crown asset. This episode should make the reader angry at polite institutions.

## Main Locations

- East Recovery Room
- Veyrhold administrative hearing chamber
- Infirmary corridor
- Student notice board outside Bellweather Hall

## Major Characters

- Jake Hargrath
- Reina Varros
- Tamsin Cael
- Professor Halvek
- Headmistress Marran
- Master Cadrin Voss
- Mistress Olwen Rusk, familiar ethics teacher
- Pell Cevran
- Miri Sollen

## A Plot — The Provisional Status Hearing

Jake is brought before a closed academy review. The language is civilized and disgusting: “hazardous autonomy,” “summoner liability,” “familiar classification,” “extra-mundane entity,” and “provisional personhood.” Reina is expected to answer questions about the bond as if she deliberately created it. Jake realizes the hearing is less concerned with what he is than with who can claim responsibility for him.

### Key Beats

1. Tamsin insists Jake attend clothed, unchained, and addressed by name.
2. Olwen Rusk explains standard familiar law, clearly uncomfortable applying it to Jake.
3. Voss argues for security classification.
4. Halvek argues that Jake is sapient and unknown, which is apparently an inconvenient combination.
5. Marran grants Jake provisional personhood but leaves the bond legally intact.
6. Reina is declared legally responsible for Jake's actions until further review.
7. Jake says, quietly, “You people made slavery sound like paperwork.”

## B Plot — Reina as Public Contamination

Students react to the gossip sheets. Some fear Reina; others court her. Cael watches from a distance, irritated that Reina's catastrophe has made her more socially important than his competence.

### Key Beats

1. Pell's gossip sheet spreads.
2. Sella tries to apologize for giving Reina ember-salt.
3. Reina is invited to sit at a better table and refuses awkwardly.
4. Miri explains that Reina is now dangerous socially, not just magically.

## C Plot — First Earth Reference in Records

Halvek begins a private search in the restricted stacks. He finds references to “extra-mundane road failure,” “unclaimed vessel,” and a redacted word beginning with “Ea—”. He closes the book when Archivist Pellan approaches.

## Foreshadowing

- “Unclaimed” appears in legal language.
- Familiar law mirrors divine contract logic.
- Redacted Earth references imply prior contact.
- Marran knows which terms to avoid.

## Open Loops

- Who has the authority to classify Jake?
- Why does familiar law contain terms for extra-mundane beings?
- What does “unclaimed” mean beyond ordinary law?

## Episode End Hook

Reina signs a care-obligation document under protest. Jake watches her sign and says nothing. That silence hurts more than anger.

# Chapter / Episode 5 — Anatomy of a Guardian

## Episode Function

Establish Jake's body, healing, pain, ancient guardian template, and Tamsin's ethical role. Begin the emotional intimacy between Jake and Tamsin through medical care.

## Main Locations

- Tamsin's infirmary wing
- Private examination chamber
- Restricted anatomical archive
- Infirmary garden

## Major Characters

- Jake
- Tamsin
- Reina
- Halvek
- Marran
- Senior Researcher Velis Thorne
- Miri

## A Plot — Medical Examination

Tamsin conducts a full examination of Jake's new body. The episode should be intimate but clinical: pulse, reflexes, regenerative response, fire-channel mapping, sensory overload, claws, tail coordination, heat tolerance, and nutritional needs.

### Key Beats

1. Tamsin asks permission before every touch.
2. Jake mocks the absurdity of having a tail, then nearly falls when it affects his balance.
3. Tamsin discovers his healing is not painless; it is cellular violence in reverse.
4. Senior Researcher Velis requests renewable tissue samples because Jake regenerates.
5. Tamsin throws him out.
6. Jake begins trusting Tamsin because she treats his pain as his, not academy property.

## B Plot — Reina Learns Not to Watch Everything

Reina is permitted nearby but not inside the whole exam. She feels flashes through the bond anyway: embarrassment, pain, anger, and a humiliating awareness of Jake's body as body, not abstraction.

### Key Beats

1. Reina starts to enter without asking.
2. Tamsin stops her and explains privacy.
3. Reina feels Jake's discomfort and realizes her presence can worsen it.
4. Miri tells Reina that guilt is not a visitor's pass.

## C Plot — Guardian Anatomy

Halvek compares Jake's measurements to redacted guardian-kith records. The old records are incomplete, and some pages have been deliberately burned.

## Foreshadowing

- Jake's body resembles an extinct road-warden species.
- His regeneration is tied to Life but consumes immense fuel.
- Fire channels resemble offering pathways, not normal elemental conduits.
- Tamsin's consent-first care contrasts with later institutional exploitation.

## Open Loops

- Why were guardian-kith records destroyed?
- Did Zoe create Jake's body or restore an old template?
- What happens if Jake's healing runs out of fuel?

## Episode End Hook

Tamsin finds an old sketch of a black guardian kith standing in a broken circle. Someone has scratched out the figure's face.

# Chapter / Episode 6 — The Command Test

## Episode Function

Make the bond's consent horror undeniable. This chapter is central to the trilogy's moral architecture. It must hurt.

## Main Locations

- Summoning Hall auxiliary chamber
- Observation gallery
- Reina's temporary infirmary room
- War Chapel exterior

## Major Characters

- Jake
- Reina
- Tamsin
- Halvek
- Marran
- Voss
- Mistress Olwen Rusk
- Miri
- Novice Vaun

## A Plot — Safety Demonstration

The academy requires proof that Reina can stop Jake in an emergency. Reina is told to issue a simple command. Jake tells her not to. Marran insists the academy cannot allow an untested strategic threat to move freely.

### Key Beats

1. Olwen frames the test as standard familiar safety protocol.
2. Tamsin objects that standard protocols do not apply to a sapient adult.
3. Jake asks Reina not to do it.
4. Reina, cornered by authority and fear, commands him to kneel.
5. Jake kneels.
6. Everyone sees it worked.
7. Jake withdraws completely.

## B Plot — Reina's Shame

Reina apologizes repeatedly. Jake finally tells her, “Don't make your guilt my job.” This becomes a defining wound between them.

### Key Beats

1. Reina tries to explain that she was scared.
2. Jake says he knows, which does not absolve her.
3. Miri tells Reina that apology can become another demand if she uses it to force forgiveness.

## C Plot — War Notices Obedience and Resistance

Novice Vaun witnesses the test from the chapel-side passage. The War Chapel bell gives one soft, unprompted chime when Jake resists the command before kneeling.

## Foreshadowing

- The bond must eventually be severed.
- War values Jake's resistance, not his obedience.
- Reina's love arc must begin with understanding the horror of command.

## Open Loops

- Can Jake resist a direct command?
- What would happen if Reina commanded him in battle?
- Why did War respond to resistance rather than compliance?

## Episode End Hook

Jake kneels alone in the infirmary garden later, voluntarily this time, testing whether the posture still belongs to him. Tamsin sees but does not interrupt.
