# Book 1 Detailed Outline — The Failed Summon

## Book Function

Book 1 is the academy entry point. It introduces Reina’s insecurity, Jake’s rebirth, the familiar bond as both comfort and violation, Veyrhold’s hidden age, Zoe’s intervention, Fire and War’s early claims, and the first false hope that Jake may one day go home.

The visible story is an isekai school adventure: a weak first-year summons a terrifying guardian instead of a fire wisp and must survive the academy’s attempts to classify, control, study, fear, and use him.

The hidden story is older and uglier: Reina’s Varros blood and Veyrhold’s buried road infrastructure reach Earth at Jake’s death-edge moment. Zoe preserves him. War and Fire mark him. The pantheon begins testing whether Jake and Reina can become the components of a future Confluence circle.

## Updated Romance Spine

Jake and Tamsin become emotionally and physically involved in Book 1 after trust forms through medical care, pain, adult loneliness, and Tamsin’s refusal to dehumanize him. Reina feels enough through the familiar bond to make the intimacy ethically impossible. Jake realizes this and breaks off further physical activity. Tamsin understands the logic but remains hurt. Reina is jealous, ashamed, curious, guilty, and secretly relieved. This event proves the bond is not merely inconvenient; it is invasive and eventually must be severed.

Jake does not reciprocate Reina’s romantic/physical interest in Book 1. Reina’s attachment is a mix of affection, dependency, adolescent infatuation, guilt, and bond-amplified need.

## Major Book 1 Characters

- **Reina Varros:** white kith first-year, failed summoner, hidden world-architecture talent.
- **Jake Hargrath:** Earth-born Marine reborn as ancient black guardian kith.
- **Tamsin Cael:** healer/anatomist; Jake’s first adult romantic partner in Veyr.
- **Zoe:** goddess of Life appearing as a child.
- **Professor Odran Halvek:** summoning professor; frightened keeper of partial truths.
- **Headmistress Ysilde Marran:** institutional protector and secret-keeper.
- **Miri Sollen:** Reina’s roommate and conscience.
- **Cael Orros:** noble duelist, early rival, future reluctant ally.
- **Sella Vey:** fire-wisp foil and ordinary-success contrast.
- **Pell Cevran:** gossip writer and future propagandist.
- **Master Cadrin Voss:** academy security head.
- **Novice Vaun:** early War observer.
- **Sera of the Ember Choir:** late cameo/foreshadowing for Book 2 fire training.

## Chapter 1 — The Lowest Fire

**Episode Function:** Introduce Reina, Veyrhold Academy, first-year social hierarchy, summoning basics, Halvek’s fear, Zoe’s first appearance, and the forbidden road stirring under a basic fire-wisp practical.

**Locations:** First Flame Court, East Spindle Hall, dining hall, Summoning Hall.  
**Characters:** Reina, Miri, Cael, Sella, Pell, Halvek, Marran, Zoe.

**A Plot:** Reina must pass the beginner fire-wisp summoning practical. Her candle fails in the First Flame exercise, and her formal summoning circle begins rotating against standard direction.  
**B Plot:** Reina navigates humiliation, Miri’s defensive humor, Cael’s polished cruelty, and Sella’s kind but painful competence.  
**C Plot:** Halvek notices Reina’s circle aligning with older brass beneath the floor and says, “That road is closed.” Zoe appears by a sealed door.

**Key Events:** First Flame failure; breakfast politics; circle inspection; Sella and Cael succeed; Reina’s circle drains heat; War Chapel bell answers; a burning black-clawed hand punches through reality.

**Foreshadowing:** smoke ring; closed-road phrase; brass floor; Zoe’s presence; War bell; Earth smell of rain on hot pavement.  
**Open Loops:** What road? Who is the child? What is coming through?

## Chapter 2 — The Man in the Circle

**Episode Function:** Complete Jake’s horrific arrival and bind him to Reina.

**Locations:** Summoning Hall, infirmary corridor, sealed observation gallery.  
**Characters:** Jake, Reina, Halvek, Marran, Voss, Tamsin, Zoe, students.

**A Plot:** Jake is dragged through the circle half-dead, burning, speaking English before translation magic clamps down. His new body dies and heals repeatedly as the familiar bond completes.  
**B Plot:** Reina experiences Jake’s pain and terror through the forming bond, realizing she has not summoned a thing but a person.  
**C Plot:** Zoe watches as Life stabilizes Jake while Fire marks him and the War Chapel bell tolls again.

**Key Events:** Jake’s body forms; students panic; Halvek tries to break the ritual; Reina cannot; Jake’s first words are fragmented Marine confusion; the bond seals.

**Foreshadowing:** Life/Fire/War all touch Jake during rebirth; translation magic feels like choking; the circle behaves like a wound, not a door.  
**Open Loops:** What was Jake doing on Earth? Why did Zoe save him? What exactly is the bond?

## Chapter 3 — Containment Protocol

**Episode Function:** Establish the academy’s first instinct: containment, not compassion.

**Locations:** Summoning Hall, containment chamber, security ward corridor.  
**Characters:** Jake, Reina, Voss, Halvek, Marran, Tamsin.

**A Plot:** Voss restrains Jake with silver-thread netting, binding rods, and sleep sigils. Jake wakes, panics, nearly kills wardens, then notices students and forces himself still.  
**B Plot:** Reina is told she may be legally responsible for Jake’s actions; she asks if he is hurt while officials ask if she can command him.  
**C Plot:** Marran orders summoning records sealed. Halvek quietly removes one page before the scribes arrive.

**Foreshadowing:** The academy already has protocols for impossible summons; Halvek’s stolen page becomes part of the return-circle theory.  
**Open Loops:** What has Veyrhold contained before? Why does Halvek steal the page?

## Chapter 4 — Property with a Pulse

**Episode Function:** Introduce familiar law and the ethical horror of Jake’s status.

**Locations:** Tribunal classroom, infirmary wing, Reina’s dorm.  
**Characters:** Jake, Reina, Tamsin, Halvek, Marran, legal clerks, Miri.

**A Plot:** A legal review provisionally classifies Jake as Reina’s familiar under hazardous extra-mundane status. Tests confirm command channel, sensory proximity, and care obligation.  
**B Plot:** Reina apologizes for owning him; Jake says intent does not unlock a cage.  
**C Plot:** A temple clerk records Jake as an intervention event and sends notice to pantheon authorities.

**Foreshadowing:** “Unclaimed extra-mundane asset” enters the legal vocabulary; familiar law mirrors divine contract law.  
**Open Loops:** Can Jake be legally declared a person? Who receives the temple notice?

## Chapter 5 — Anatomy of a Guardian

**Episode Function:** Make Tamsin central and establish medical intimacy, Jake’s body horror, and guardian-kith mystery.

**Locations:** Tamsin’s infirmary wing, anatomical archive.  
**Characters:** Jake, Tamsin, Reina, Halvek, senior researchers.

**A Plot:** Tamsin examines Jake’s healing, reinforcement reflexes, claws, fire channels, and metabolism. A researcher proposes repeated tissue sampling because Jake regenerates; Tamsin shuts it down.  
**B Plot:** Jake asks Reina to leave during parts of the exam to preserve privacy. Reina is hurt but begins understanding that access is not consent.  
**C Plot:** Tamsin compares Jake to redacted sketches of extinct guardian kith.

**Foreshadowing:** Jake’s body is an old template, not random transformation; healing is painful and fuel-hungry.  
**Open Loops:** Who erased guardian-kith records? What did Zoe rebuild?

## Chapter 6 — The Child by the Window

**Episode Function:** Zoe speaks to Jake and confirms divine weirdness without explaining it. Tamsin/Jake trust begins.

**Locations:** Infirmary recovery room, upper infirmary window, chapel corridor.  
**Characters:** Jake, Tamsin, Zoe, Reina briefly.

**A Plot:** Jake sees Zoe standing outside an impossible window. She tells him she gave him enough life to be angry about it.  
**B Plot:** Tamsin treats Jake’s pain seriously and refuses to let others treat him as a specimen. Jake begins trusting her competence.  
**C Plot:** Zoe hums a children’s song about two skies sharing one moon.

**Foreshadowing:** Two-skies song points to Confluence; Zoe saved Jake but will not say why.  
**Open Loops:** Is Zoe visible to others? Why does she appear as a child?

## Chapter 7 — Roommate of the Monster Girl

**Episode Function:** Return to student slice of life and show gossip spreading.

**Locations:** East Spindle Hall, dining hall, student notice wall.  
**Characters:** Reina, Miri, Pell, Sella, Cael, Jessa Flinttail.

**A Plot:** Reina returns to dorm life under guard while students stare, fear, flatter, and speculate. Pell’s anonymous sheet names Jake “the Black Familiar.”  
**B Plot:** Reina confesses she can still feel Jake’s pain and hunger. Miri warns that this is not romance; it is trespassing with feelings.  
**C Plot:** A temple courier collects gossip sheets and sends them outside Veyrhold.

**Foreshadowing:** Gossip becomes propaganda in Book 2 and ritual belief in Book 3.  
**Open Loops:** Why does the temple care about student gossip?

## Chapter 8 — First Command

**Episode Function:** Make the bond’s violation undeniable.

**Locations:** Safety ward chamber, observation gallery.  
**Characters:** Jake, Reina, Marran, Halvek, Voss, Tamsin, academy witnesses.

**A Plot:** The academy requires a safety demonstration. Reina must command Jake. Under pressure, she commands him to kneel. He does, unwillingly.  
**B Plot:** Jake withdraws rather than rage. Reina apologizes until apology becomes a burden. He says, “Don’t make your guilt my job.”  
**C Plot:** Halvek records that the command channel appears grafted onto a soul not designed for familiar binding.

**Foreshadowing:** Moral reason for eventual severance; Jake’s soul is incompatible with being claimed.  
**Open Loops:** Can the command channel be removed?

## Chapter 9 — Duel Day

**Episode Function:** Show school hierarchy becoming physically dangerous and Jake’s protective reflex as both gift and problem.

**Locations:** Duel Lawns, infirmary, student gallery.  
**Characters:** Reina, Jake, Cael, Miri, Sella, Vaun, Tamsin.

**A Plot:** Cael challenges Reina in an informal duel framed as testing whether she can control her beast. His wind-serpent strikes Reina; Jake moves before anyone can stop him and pins the serpent without killing Cael.  
**B Plot:** Reina is humiliated by needing rescue. Jake is angry the bond dragged him toward violence. Cael is humiliated by being spared.  
**C Plot:** Novice Vaun watches from the chapel balcony and marks Jake with War’s sign.

**Foreshadowing:** Protective reflex will be exploited in Book 2; War is watching restraint as much as violence.  
**Open Loops:** Why does Vaun care?

## Chapter 10 — The Wrong Kind of Fire

**Episode Function:** Establish Jake’s fire as abnormal, dangerous, and tied to emotion.

**Locations:** Fire-control lab, Cinder Steps shrine overlook.  
**Characters:** Jake, Tamsin, Reina, Halvek, Sera cameo, Fire temple observers.

**A Plot:** During testing, Jake’s anger ignites ember channels under his fur and nearly burns through containment sigils.  
**B Plot:** Reina feels rage, pain, hunger, and a troubling thrill through the bond. She is frightened by liking the power.  
**C Plot:** Sera of the Ember Choir sees the flare and says it is not wisp-line fire.

**Foreshadowing:** Fire is offering and hunger, not simple elemental affinity.  
**Open Loops:** What does Sera know about Jake’s fire?

## Chapter 11 — Varros Blood

**Episode Function:** Bring Reina’s family pressure into the plot.

**Locations:** dormitory, message hall, Halvek’s office.  
**Characters:** Reina, Miri, Jake, Halvek, Lysa by letter, House Varros envoys.

**A Plot:** House Varros sends inquiry letters asking whether Jake can restore family prestige. Lysa privately warns Reina not to trust sudden affection.  
**B Plot:** Jake bluntly identifies her family’s interest as usefulness, not love. Reina hates that he is right.  
**C Plot:** Halvek studies Reina’s original circle and finds old Varros notation hidden inside standard academy diagramming.

**Foreshadowing:** Varros blood is old-road compatible.  
**Open Loops:** Why did House Varros preserve forbidden geometry?

## Chapter 12 — The Locked Stair

**Episode Function:** Give the first adventure-mystery descent into Veyrhold’s buried history.

**Locations:** Restricted Stacks, Locked Stair, under-library mural chamber.  
**Characters:** Reina, Miri, Jake, Halvek/Archivist Pellan in pursuit.

**A Plot:** Miri and Reina sneak into restricted stacks to research guardian kith. Jake follows because Reina’s fear tugs the bond. They find the Locked Stair.  
**B Plot:** Jake tells Reina fragments of Earth: coffee, rifles, rain on asphalt, bad MREs. Their first almost-easy conversation happens while trespassing, because naturally humans and kith both bond through bad decisions.  
**C Plot:** Beneath the library they find a damaged mural of black kith figures and smaller pale figures under two moons and a blue-white sphere Jake recognizes as Earth.

**Foreshadowing:** Earth and Veyr were connected; academy built over older road infrastructure.  
**Open Loops:** Why is Earth painted under Veyrhold?

## Chapter 13 — Punishments and Other Lessons

**Episode Function:** Show institutional control and Marran’s partial knowledge.

**Locations:** Headmistress office, work-detail corridors, infirmary.  
**Characters:** Reina, Jake, Miri, Marran, Halvek, Tamsin.

**A Plot:** Reina, Miri, and Jake are punished for restricted access. Marran privately questions Jake about Earth and knows he lies badly.  
**B Plot:** Jake and Reina argue over curiosity versus survival; Miri tells them both they are terrible at friendship.  
**C Plot:** Marran orders Halvek to stop investigating pre-academy ruins. He agrees and immediately does not.

**Foreshadowing:** Marran knows Earth-linked history exists but protects institutional secrecy over truth.  
**Open Loops:** What does Marran know?

## Chapter 14 — Familiar Court

**Episode Function:** Formalize Jake’s status and social danger.

**Locations:** academy tribunal hall, public gallery, legal archive.  
**Characters:** Jake, Reina, Tamsin, Marran, Halvek, Voss, legal clerks, noble observers, temple clerk.

**A Plot:** Tribunal debates Jake as familiar, intelligent summoned person, divine omen, dangerous beast, crown property, or research subject. He receives provisional autonomy with the bond legally intact.  
**B Plot:** Reina publicly states Jake is not property, costing her status. Jake believes her in the moment but cannot forget the command test.  
**C Plot:** A clerk uses the phrase “unclaimed extra-mundane asset,” and Halvek reacts.

**Foreshadowing:** “Unclaimed” will matter in Book 3; legal compromise leaves the core wound intact.  
**Open Loops:** Who wants Jake as crown property?

## Chapter 15 — The Chapel Bell Rings Twice

**Episode Function:** Deepen War’s interest.

**Locations:** War Chapel, Bell Court, dormitory corridor.  
**Characters:** Jake, Vaun, Reina, Miri.

**A Plot:** Jake is drawn to the War Chapel after nightmares of Earth and unknown battlefields. The bell rings when he enters. Vaun tells him bells ring for the dead, the called, and the chosen.  
**B Plot:** Reina follows but remains outside after Jake asks for space. It is her first real act of non-possessive care.  
**C Plot:** Vaun dreams of a black-furred soldier on a bridge between two burning skies and reports it to the War temple.

**Foreshadowing:** Jake as bridge-holder and future ignition point.  
**Open Loops:** Chosen by whom, and for what?

## Chapter 16 — Teeth in the Glasshouse

**Episode Function:** First major action episode after the summoning; show Jake’s violence, restraint, and cost.

**Locations:** Glasshouse, infirmary, security yard.  
**Characters:** Jake, Reina, Tamsin, Voss, Sella, students, feral familiar.

**A Plot:** An experimental familiar goes feral after exposure to residual summoning magic. Students are trapped. Jake fights it in close quarters, takes horrific injuries, and saves most students, but one dies.  
**B Plot:** Reina feels Jake’s wounds and the student’s death through him but lies when asked. Tamsin realizes the bond can transmit trauma.  
**C Plot:** The creature’s body shows crude blackened fire-channel patterns similar to Jake’s.

**Foreshadowing:** Jake’s arrival contaminates local magic; later bond fever and world bleed begin in miniature.  
**Open Loops:** Is the summoning site still active?

## Chapter 17 — Pell’s Monster Sheet

**Episode Function:** Turn heroism into public narrative and shame.

**Locations:** dining hall, notice wall, dormitory, infirmary.  
**Characters:** Reina, Miri, Pell, Jake, Cael, Jessa, Tamsin.

**A Plot:** Pell publishes sensational versions of the glasshouse fight: Jake as hero, demon, beast, and leash-bound familiar. School factions form.  
**B Plot:** Reina nearly retaliates socially against students spreading obscene leash gossip. Miri stops her from becoming what they accuse her of being.  
**C Plot:** A temple courier again collects copies.

**Foreshadowing:** Stories about Jake are being harvested.  
**Open Loops:** Why does every rumor leave campus?

## Chapter 18 — Lessons in Not Touching

**Episode Function:** Establish Tamsin’s ethics and build the Jake/Tamsin romantic trajectory.

**Locations:** infirmary, recovery garden, Tamsin’s office.  
**Characters:** Jake, Tamsin, Reina, Marran, senior researchers.

**A Plot:** Tamsin imposes care protocols: ask before touching, no restraints without explanation, no command testing without consent, no pain experiments.  
**B Plot:** Reina asks Tamsin how to help Jake without using the bond. Tamsin says trust cannot be built from access Reina did not earn. Jake witnesses this and respects Tamsin more.  
**C Plot:** Tamsin observes Jake’s healing changes when Reina is near, but not always positively.

**Foreshadowing:** Bond affects biology and must eventually be severed. Tamsin’s ethical intimacy lays groundwork for romance.  
**Open Loops:** Can Reina learn care without possession?

## Chapter 19 — The Fire Wisp That Finally Came

**Episode Function:** Reina passes the original test but learns success is no longer enough.

**Locations:** Summoning Hall, Halvek’s office, dormitory.  
**Characters:** Reina, Halvek, Jake, Miri, Sella, students.

**A Plot:** Reina retakes the fire-wisp practical. She summons a tiny wisp. It flees from Jake, then circles Reina as a ring instead of a flame. Halvek stops the test.  
**B Plot:** Reina feels hollow rather than triumphant. Jake congratulates her for not needing the wisp to prove she exists.  
**C Plot:** Halvek matches the wisp’s ring-shape to old diagrams he cannot fully identify.

**Foreshadowing:** Reina’s true magic forms rings and anchors, not ordinary elemental summons.  
**Open Loops:** Why did the wisp become a circle?

## Chapter 20 — The Infirmary Door

**Episode Function:** Advance Jake/Tamsin romance and make Reina’s bond-access emotionally dangerous before physical intimacy occurs.

**Locations:** infirmary, late-night corridor, recovery garden.  
**Characters:** Jake, Tamsin, Reina, Miri.

**A Plot:** After a regeneration crisis, Tamsin stays with Jake through the night. They talk honestly about pain, loneliness, Earth, and the absurdity of surviving only to be inventoried by academics. Attraction becomes explicit but restrained.  
**B Plot:** Reina wakes in the dorm feeling warmth, relief, and longing that are not hers. She does not fully understand what she is feeling, only that Jake is emotionally closer to someone else.  
**C Plot:** Zoe appears briefly in the recovery garden and warns Jake that life grows toward warmth, even when warmth burns.

**Foreshadowing:** The bond can leak attraction before anyone crosses a line; Zoe knows intimacy will become a pressure point.  
**Open Loops:** Will Jake and Tamsin act on it? How much can Reina feel?

## Chapter 21 — The Night Market Breach

**Episode Function:** External danger enters public space and the old-road cult language appears.

**Locations:** Lanternmarket, alleyways, rooftop shrine, infirmary.  
**Characters:** Jake, Reina, Miri, Cael, Sella, Ma Dorel, masked attackers, Voss.

**A Plot:** A supervised city visit becomes an abduction attempt on Reina. Masked attackers use anti-familiar wards and Earth-symbol charms. Jake tears through the ward by feeding blood into his fire channels.  
**B Plot:** Reina sees civilians fear and worship Jake after he saves them. She is both proud and horrified.  
**C Plot:** A captured attacker kills himself after saying, “The road opens where blood remembers.”

**Foreshadowing:** Old-road cults know something; blood opens roads.  
**Open Loops:** Who sent the attackers? Why target Reina?

## Chapter 22 — The Headmistress’s Lie

**Episode Function:** Expose institutional lying and prepare the underground turn.

**Locations:** assembly hall, headmistress office, Low Warrens entrance.  
**Characters:** Marran, Jake, Reina, Miri, Halvek, Voss.

**A Plot:** Marran publicly calls the attack anti-academy extremism. Jake confronts her, recognizing a partial lie. She admits some groups believe old divine roads are reopening.  
**B Plot:** Reina learns adults lie to preserve systems, not merely avoid punishment. Miri calls that rot with a pension, because apparently subtlety died in committee.  
**C Plot:** Marran seals old catacomb entrances; something beneath the academy opens a different door.

**Foreshadowing:** Roads cannot be sealed by ordinary authority.  
**Open Loops:** What opened under Veyrhold?

## Chapter 23 — Under Veyrhold

**Episode Function:** Adventure episode revealing Earth artifacts and the old chamber.

**Locations:** Underhold catacombs, sealed circular chamber, old service tunnels.  
**Characters:** Jake, Reina, Miri, Cael, Sella, Voss.

**A Plot:** A wardquake traps Jake, Reina, Miri, Cael, and Sella underground. They navigate catacombs and find old summoning platforms and non-modern skeletal remains. Jake finds a corroded Earth coin or brass casing.  
**B Plot:** Cael breaks down and admits he resented Reina because her failure made her special while his success made him replaceable.  
**C Plot:** They find a circular chamber whose depression matches Reina’s original summoning circle. It hums when Jake steps near.

**Foreshadowing:** Veyrhold stands over transit-root infrastructure; Earth objects prove earlier crossings.  
**Open Loops:** How did Earth material get under the academy?

## Chapter 24 — The Bond in the Dark

**Episode Function:** Bring Jake/Tamsin physical romance to its ethical crisis.

**Locations:** infirmary private room, dormitory, corridor outside Tamsin’s office.  
**Characters:** Jake, Tamsin, Reina, Miri, Zoe briefly.

**A Plot:** After the Underhold injuries, Tamsin treats Jake late at night. Exhaustion, vulnerability, and mutual desire finally break restraint. Jake and Tamsin begin physical intimacy.  
**B Plot:** Reina feels flashes through the bond: warmth, touch, desire, and Jake’s attempt to keep his mind shuttered. She is overwhelmed by jealousy, shame, curiosity, and humiliation. Miri finds her panicking.  
**C Plot:** Jake realizes Reina is feeling enough to be present in the moment without consent. He stops immediately and breaks off further physical activity with Tamsin.

**Foreshadowing:** The bond contaminates adult intimacy and makes severance morally necessary. Tamsin’s lingering hurt becomes Book 2 relapse fuel.  
**Open Loops:** Can Jake and Tamsin recover? Can Reina live with what she felt? Can any privacy exist while the bond remains?

## Chapter 25 — A Door Drawn in Ash

**Episode Function:** End Book 1 with raid, first return theory, exposed infrastructure, and war approaching.

**Locations:** Summoning Hall, Restricted Archive, Underhold chamber, Bell Court.  
**Characters:** Jake, Reina, Tamsin, Halvek, Marran, Voss, Cael, Miri, Sella, Zoe, masked attackers.

**A Plot:** A coordinated raid targets Reina’s original summoning hall, the exposed Underhold chamber, and the restricted archive. Jake defends first-years as fire channels blaze, looking demonic while saving children.  
**B Plot:** Reina refuses to command Jake even when doing so might make the fight easier. She asks him to hold the hall. He chooses to do it. Tamsin treats his injuries afterward, their rupture still raw.  
**C Plot:** Halvek, Tamsin, and Marran compare the exposed chamber, burned Transit Root diagram, Jake’s fire residue, and Reina’s original marks. Halvek says there may be a theoretical way to reverse-engineer the summoning and send Jake home someday.

**Key Events:** archive theft attempt; Cael injured protecting Reina; Jake kills an infiltrator efficiently; Zoe closes a dying attacker’s eyes as he whispers, “Life was in on it”; temple bells ring across provinces; dispatch mentions High Marshal Soreth Vaul.

**Foreshadowing:** The “return circle” is actually misread Confluence geometry; Zoe is complicit; war is moving toward Veyrhold.  
**Open Loops:** Can Jake go home? What did Life help start? Why are temples ringing? What does Soreth’s arrival mean?

## Final Book 1 State

- Jake is provisionally autonomous but still bonded to Reina.
- Reina has chosen not to command him in crisis, but her dependency remains unresolved.
- Jake and Tamsin’s relationship is real, interrupted, and emotionally unfinished.
- Tamsin knows the bond is medically and ethically dangerous.
- Veyrhold’s old road infrastructure has been exposed.
- Halvek has introduced the first theory of a way home.
- Outside factions know Jake and Reina matter.
- Zoe has shown care and complicity.
- War and Fire have both marked Jake.
- Book 2 can begin with the academy draft and the arrival of wartime command.
